We've got your request for a discovery peer review for {{data['title']}}

An email has been sent to you confirming the details of your request. You'll also be able to find this on the My requests page.

What happens next

  1. The Service Assurance Team will review the request

    They'll check it's not a duplicate, and assign relevant spend control information to the request.

  2. If accepted

    You'll get an email with a link to your request to select a date for the review to take place.

    Once you've selected a date, the Service Assurance Team will get together a peer review panel.

    You can view the overall progress of your request in this service on the My requests page.

    You will get emails asking you to provide additional information nearer to the peer review, such as links to any presentations or artefacts to support the peer review process.

  3. If rejected

    You'll get an email giving details of why it's been rejected. This could be because it's a duplicate request or a peer review may not be appropriate.

    You'll be told of any next steps or what action to take.
